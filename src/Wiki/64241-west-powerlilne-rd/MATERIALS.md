### Recommended Color Scheme for Aunt Liz's House

**Option 1: Modern Neutral (Most Popular)**
- Main walls: Agreeable Gray (SW 7029) or Edgecomb Gray (BM HC-173)
- Trim/doors: White Dove (BM OC-17) or Alabaster (SW 7008)
- Ceilings: Same as trim, or flat white
- Cabinets: White Dove (BM OC-17) for brightness

**Option 2: Warm & Inviting**
- Main walls: Accessible Beige (SW 7036) or Revere Pewter (BM HC-172)
- Trim/doors: Simply White (BM OC-117)
- Ceilings: Simply White
- Cabinets: White or keep natural wood if good condition

**Option 3: Light & Bright**
- Main walls: Alabaster (SW 7008) throughout
- Trim/doors: Pure White (SW 7005)
- Ceilings: Pure White
- Cabinets: White Dove (BM OC-17)

**RECOMMENDATION:** Option 1 (Modern Neutral)
- Most broadly appealing
- Photographs extremely well
- Works with any buyer's furniture
- Agreeable Gray is consistently top-rated for resale

### Paint Finish Guide
- **Flat/Matte:** Ceilings, low-traffic walls (hides imperfections)
- **Eggshell:** Main walls (easy to clean, slight sheen)
- **Satin:** Bathrooms, kitchens, high-traffic areas (more durable)
- **Semi-gloss:** Trim, doors, cabinets (very durable, washable)
- **Gloss:** Not recommended for resale (too shiny)

## Carpet Selection

### Carpet Types for Resale

| Type | Durability | Feel | Cost/sq ft | Best For | Notes |
|------|------------|------|------------|----------|-------|
| **Nylon** | Excellent | Soft | $3-8 | Bedrooms, high traffic | Most popular, best choice |
| **Polyester** | Good | Very soft | $2-6 | Bedrooms | Budget-friendly |
| **Triexta** | Excellent | Soft | $4-7 | All areas | Stain-resistant |
| **Wool** | Excellent | Luxurious | $8-20 | Premium homes | Too expensive for resale |

**RECOMMENDATION:** Nylon carpet with stain protection
- Best balance of durability, feel, and cost
- Long-lasting appearance
- Easy to clean

### Carpet Colors for Resale

**Top Neutral Colors:**
1. **Beige/Tan** - Classic, warm, hides dirt well
2. **Light Gray** - Modern, popular with younger buyers
3. **Greige (Gray-Beige)** - Perfect compromise, very trendy
4. **Soft Taupe** - Sophisticated neutral
5. **Light Brown** - Warm and inviting

**Colors to AVOID:**
- White/Cream (shows every stain)
- Dark colors (makes rooms feel smaller)
- Bold colors (limits buyer appeal)
- Patterns (too personal)

**RECOMMENDATION:** Medium-tone greige or soft beige
- Coordinates with gray OR beige walls
- Photographs well
- Broadest appeal

## Back Door Selection

### Door Material Options

| Material | Pros | Cons | Cost Range | Best For |
|----------|------|------|------------|----------|
| **Fiberglass** | Low maintenance, energy efficient, looks like wood | More expensive | $300-600 | Best overall value |
| **Steel** | Affordable, secure, energy efficient | Can dent, needs repainting | $200-500 | Budget choice |
| **Wood** | Beautiful, traditional | High maintenance, warps | $500-1,200 | Premium homes only |

**RECOMMENDATION:** Fiberglass door
- Looks upscale without high cost
- Minimal maintenance (important for resale)
- Energy efficient (selling point)
- Won't warp or rot

### Door Style Recommendations

**For Resale:** Choose simple, classic designs
- 6-panel traditional style (most universal)
- Clean, simple glass insert if back door needs light
- White or neutral color
- Quality hardware (brushed nickel or oil-rubbed bronze)

**Popular Options:**
- Masonite or Therma-Tru fiberglass 6-panel
- Half-glass insert if more light needed
- Pre-hung for easier installation

**Estimated Cost:** $400-800 (door + installation)
